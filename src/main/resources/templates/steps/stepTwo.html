Changing the Tire

Consult the owner's manuel for more specific instructions regarding your vehicle, and for the location of a portable carjack if provided. However, if none are present, ensure you have access to the correct tool for the job, namely a "lug wrench" or "tire iron". In the United Kingdom and Australia, it is known as a wheel brace. All of which is a type of socket wrench to loosening or tightening lug nuts on the wheels of your vehicles.

Ensure your vehicle is parked and turned off, preferably with the parking break engaged. Then proceed to utilize the provided carjack to lift the tire in need of replacment off the road by orientating the carjack beneath the axel or suspension nearest the wheel.

Proceed to loosen each lug nut, but not fully remove them until all are loosened. This prevents the tire from wobbling around too much while working on the nuts. Then proceed to remove all the nuts, place them in a safe location, then remove the actual tire.
